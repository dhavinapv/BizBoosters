History of Uniqlo

Uniqlo is a Japanese casual wear brand founded in 1984 by Tadashi Yanai. The name Uniqlo is an acronym for “Unique Clothing,” which represents a focus on simple, comfortable, and quality casual clothing. The first store opened in Hiroshima, Japan, and since then the brand has grown rapidly through the “SPA” (Specialty Store Retailer of Private Label Apparel) concept, which allows Uniqlo to control the entire production process to keep quality and prices competitive.

In 2001, Uniqlo began its international expansion by opening a store in London, followed by its presence in various countries including Indonesia in 2013. The brand is known for its innovative material technologies such as Heattech and AIRism, which create functional clothing for various weather conditions. With the philosophy of “LifeWear,” Uniqlo is committed to providing relevant clothing for everyday life, prioritizing minimalism and flexibility in its designs.

Uniqlo also supports sustainability through its “Re.Uniqlo” clothing recycling program and collaborates with famous designers and artists to create unique collections. Until now, Uniqlo has become one of the largest clothing brands in the world, with more than 2,400 stores in various countries.


Vision and Mission of Uniqlo

Uniqlo's vision: To unlock the power of clothing with a focus on product, supply chain, employees, stores and communities, we will change the world for the better through our business.
Uniqlo's mission: To continuously provide fashionable, high quality, basic casual wear at the lowest pricesin the market–casual wear that anybody can wear whenever and wherever, focus on lowcost operation and directly link production and marketing by the shortest and cheapest means. Provide an environment where global standard employees can happily work.
